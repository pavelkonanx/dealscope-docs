# LinkedIn to Salesforce Import with DealScope

The public DealScope site describes a workflow for bringing LinkedIn conversations into Salesforce so they become usable record context.

## What It Does

The public page says you can copy a LinkedIn conversation into DealScope and have it turned into readable Salesforce activity history with AI summary support.

The page says the feature can:

- split the conversation by days
- create activity events
- keep the dialog readable
- generate summaries
- include the imported messages in the AI brief

## Supported Record Types

The public page describes this workflow for:

- Lead
- Opportunity

## How It Works

The public site describes this flow:

1. Copy the full LinkedIn dialog.
2. Open the DealScope panel in Salesforce.
3. Choose `Add LinkedIn dialog`.
4. Paste the text.
5. DealScope detects dates, days, participants, and messages.
6. Each day becomes a separate activity event.
7. The imported context is used in the AI summary.

## Why Teams Use It

The public page lists a common problem:

- sales reps talk in LinkedIn
- the conversation never reaches CRM
- the activity history is incomplete
- the AI brief misses important context

DealScope is positioned as the tool that closes that gap without manual logging.

## Public Compatibility Notes

The public page says this feature:

- works inside Salesforce record pages
- does not require Einstein licenses
- does not require AI add-ons

It also lists these supported org types:

- Professional
- Enterprise
- Unlimited
- Sandbox
- Dev org
